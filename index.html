Work experience

goSupermodel.com - Momio ApS

Team Leader - Lead Artist - Content Manager

goSupermodel is an Fashion Focused online media platform and web game for young adults. I was hired to lead and manage the content artists and their work tasks. At the start I was managing a team of about 14 volunteer artists and 6 paid freelancers. The team of artists drew clothes and accessories for the users to dress up their avatars with.
Later on we moved away from having a specific group of volunteer artists and opened it up for all users to send in their own creations. With this I was put in charge of quality checking all item submissions as well as giving feedback to the users whose items could not be approved of at the moment.

I also became the lead artist for creating all content for goSupermodels home decor minigame. This of course entailed drawing a lot of furniture sprite sheets that fit the already existing style and oblique graphic projection of the game. I would occasionally help out with creating clothing items and website graphics when needed as well.

I've gained a lot of new skills and knowledge from working at goSupermodel. It has taught me great leadership skills, how to interact professionally with an online userbase and customers, event managing and marketing to an online userbase, and efficient art content creation.

Education

Gymnasieexamen - IT Media

The Swedish school system differs from the American one, so here's a brief explanation of my education level and background. After year 9, Swedish students attend Gymnasiet, which could be concidered an equivalent of high school. During Gymnasiet, students choose a specialization based on their future career or study interests.

I chose the IT Media track due to my strong interest in programming and technology. My coursework included programming languages such as JavaScript, HTML, C++, and C#, along with graphic design and web development. I also took extra courses in animation and art, and graduated at age 19, top of my class in all media-related subjects.

Originally, I planned to pursue a college degree in web or game design, but I chose instead to enter the workforce and gain practical experience. Since graduating, I’ve worked continuously exploring different career paths while being a freelance artist, and continuing to grow my skills through personal projects and online courses.

While I don't hold a formal college degree, I bring hands-on experience, a strong work ethic. Through freelance work, personal projects, and self-directed learning, I’ve developed a solid foundation in digital art, animation, and game-focused visual design.

About me

My name is Elin Oskarsson, and I live in southern Sweden with my partner and our two cats, Yin and Tonic. I’m a digital artist who has been drawing since I was 15, and I’ve developed a strong foundation in concept art, character design, animation, 3D modeling and background art. I’m a quick learner, always eager to explore new styles and tools, and I truly enjoy pushing myself creatively.

I’ve worked remotely in the past and am very comfortable with working from home. Working across time zones and in English has been a normal part of my routine. As a natural night owl, I’ve always found it easy to align with American hours.

Getting to work in games has been a long-time goal of mine. Games like Sally Face have always been a big inspiration for me. The game first came out when I was 16 years old and it was games like these that inspired me to get into game design in the first place. My dream is to have the opportunity to work on creating something that resonates with players and inspires others.

With my background in digital art and hands-on experience, I bring a strong foundation in game focused visual design. I thrive in creative environments where I can contribute fresh ideas and continuously improve my skills. I hope to get the opportunity to talk more to you about the position and potentially help you bring your vision and future visions to life.

goSupermodel.com

On goSupermodel I mostly focus on creating furniture sprites for the 2D Décor the in game furnishing game. In my process for these specific furniture I often integrate Blender as a tool to help speed up the process. Drawing the same item from multiple angles can be quite time consuming so for some complicated pieces that need to have multiple rotations I can make a 3d model of it and render it in the correct projection and resolution to make it fit the size and style of the game. Then I will go in and polish the pieces further by hand to give the item extra details and make sure it looks perfect. Occasionally I will also help create wearable clothes for the users to dress their avatars in.
